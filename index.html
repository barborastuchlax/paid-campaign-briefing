How an MQL actually happens

It's not a one-click funnel. In this business it's typically 5–9 touches over 3–6 months, and most MQLs won't credit paid directly — they'll say "saw you on LinkedIn" or "a colleague mentioned you." Paid's role is to make those answers possible. Retargeting is the loop that holds the whole thing together.

Retargeting — the loop that cycles warm audiences back through

Realities this structure acknowledges

  • Attribution is fuzzy. Self-reported "how did you hear about us" will beat click-attribution for the MQLs we care about. Paid is air cover, not last-click.
  • Paid doesn't close. It primes ICP accounts for outbound, organic, and Clutch conversations to land. Awareness ads should be judged on reach and retargeting-pool size, not MQLs.
  • Retargeting is the glue. It's not a stage — it's the mechanism that cycles warm audiences back through until they're ready.
  • Outbound runs in parallel, not downstream. Our outbound list sits inside the LinkedIn Matched Audience.

Track 1Priority Verticals — what each angle does

Industry Expertise ★ (primary bet)

Leads with domain knowledge and proof. "65+ products shipped. Deep experience across fintech, cybersecurity, and healthtech. ISO-certified security practices built into the architecture from day one."

Why primary: vertical CTOs/CPOs discount generic engineering claims. They buy from partners who already know the regulatory landscape they ship in.

Compliance Expertise

ISO-certified practices. Compliance designed into the architecture, not bolted on. This angle anchors the message in proof that a CTO/CPO can verify.

AI as Accelerator

Two claims: we build AI-powered products (LLMs, RAG, agentic) and we use AI to build faster (one engineer = 2–3x a typical hire). Lands in Wave 1 as an educational carousel ("How to ship AI without hiring an ML team") — no gating, since we don't yet have a dedicated AI page or case study volume to anchor a lead-gen ask.

Positioning note: No client names in paid copy. ISO only — we don't claim SOC 2, HIPAA, or PCI-DSS certification.
Track 2SaaS + General Software — what each angle does

Audience: B2B SaaS CTOs/CPOs at 51–500-headcount companies, US, excluding fintech/cybersec/healthtech (so Track 1 audiences don't overlap). Narrower than "general tech" was — because targeted SaaS lets us lean into execution + talent + partnership claims that actually land.

The Scaling Problem

Pain-point first. "Your roadmap is ready. Your team is at capacity. Hiring takes months, and great hires are hard to find and slower to ramp." Names the problem before the solution.

Why this works here: SaaS CTOs/CPOs aren't pre-filtering on industry fit. They're filtering on "can this team actually help me ship faster without blowing out my org."

Product & Engineering Partner

Core positioning. Two delivery models (embed or autonomous), 4-year average client partnership, 5.0 Clutch rating. This is the "who we are" ad.

Elite Engineering + AI

Talent quality + AI leverage. Top 0.4% engineers, Prague-based, US-focused. Full-stack JS/TS. Ends with a proof claim about AI-accelerated delivery.

What won't be obvious in the first 30 days

  • Awareness ads won't drive direct MQLs. They're warming the audience for Wave 2 and for our outbound motion. Judging them on form fills misses the point.
  • Outbound warm-up impact is measured in outbound reply rate, not in ad metrics. That feedback loop closes 2–3 weeks into the sequences.
  • Vertical traffic is slow but valuable. Track 1 audiences are smaller; lower CTR is expected, and MQL quality should be higher.

What to push back on if you see it

  • Any vertical ad not leading with industry proof — Industry Expertise is the primary bet, not a fallback.
  • Any copy drifting toward "agency," "dev shop," or generic engineering-speed claims.
  • Client names in paid copy (rule: proof points and stats only, no named logos).
